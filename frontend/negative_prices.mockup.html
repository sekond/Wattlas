When power costs less than nothing
Sometimes there's so much wind and solar, and so little demand to soak it up, that the wholesale price falls below zero — producers pay to keep generating. Wattlas treats this as a first-class metric: how many hours a year, when they cluster, and how long each spell lasts.

2 · How long do they last?
episode duration
A short dip is easy to ride through; a long one is what hurts. As midday solar grows, the negative spells stretch from an odd hour into long, continuous afternoon runs.
Most episodes last an hour or two; a handful run nine hours or more.
An episode = a run of consecutive negative hours within the canonical hourly series. The multi-hour midday-solar runs are what experts now emphasise. Episode counts are illustrative placeholders; the yearly hour total is real.
3 · Which months go negative
negative hours per month
Negative prices are a spring-and-early-summer story: long days, strong sun, mild demand. Dark winter months barely register.
