What a megawatt-hour of solar is actually worth
As more solar floods the same midday hours, each one earns less. The capture price is the average price solar actually receives, generation-weighted. Divided by the baseload price, that's the value factor — and it's falling.

1 · The value factor, falling
Capture price ÷ baseload, monthly. A value factor of 1.0 means a fuel earns exactly the average price; below 1.0 it earns less than baseload because it produces when everyone else does. Solar (amber) cannibalizes itself fastest; wind (teal) holds up better.
Solar value factor falls toward 0.55; wind holds near 0.82.
SolarWindValue factor = 1.0
Generation and price are resampled to one canonical hourly resolution before weighting (Germany switched to quarter-hourly settlement in Oct 2025). Anchors — DE solar capture ~50–60% of baseload — are cited 2025 context, not computed here. Illustrative placeholder.
