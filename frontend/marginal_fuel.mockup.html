Why the price is what it is: the fuel on the margin
In most hours one fuel sets the wholesale price — the most expensive plant needed to meet demand. When renewables cover it, prices fall; when gas sets it, prices follow the gas (and CO2) cost. This overlay explains the geopolitics-as-price-driver story.

2 · The price follows the gas
DE-LU day-ahead vs gas-fired marginal cost
Average day-ahead price by hour against the inferred cost of running a gas plant (fuel + CO2). In non-solar hours the two track tightly — gas is on the margin, so the price is the gas cost. Around midday, solar pushes the price well below gas: renewables are setting the margin instead.

In negative-price hours renewables set the margin and the model says "renewable", not gas — the dashed gas line is what gas would cost to run, not what cleared. Gas marginal cost = (TTF ÷ efficiency) + (CO2 × emissions factor). Illustrative placeholder.
