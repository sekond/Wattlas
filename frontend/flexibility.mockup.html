What you'd save by shifting load to the cheapest hours
A dynamic tariff prices each hour at wholesale; a shiftable load — an EV, a heat pump, a home battery — that runs in the cheapest N hours per day beats a flat tariff. This calculator shows how much, per zone.

1 · A day, and its cheapest window
DE-LU day-ahead, € / MWh by hour
A typical day's prices follow a duck curve: cheap when solar floods midday, dear in the evening peak. A shiftable load charges in the cheapest contiguous window (amber) and avoids the peak — that gap is the saving.
Prices dip midday to about 30 and peak near 150 in the evening; the cheapest four midday hours are the charging window.
