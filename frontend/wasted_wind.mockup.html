2 · Surplus north, deficit south

Generation minus load per control area. 50Hertz and TenneT (north) run long; Amprion and TransnetBW (south/west) run short. When the corridor between them is full, the operator pays plants to turn down north and up south — redispatch.

DE-LU day-ahead: one price for both halves
Germany–Luxembourg is a single bidding zone: one wholesale price, even when the grid can't move power from the windy north to the industrial south. Some economists argue that splitting into smaller zones would signal scarcity and pull investment where it's needed; others say one zone preserves price solidarity between regions, avoids structurally higher southern prices, and keeps the market liquid. Wattlas takes no side — it shows the physical mismatch the debate is about.
"Wasted" = renewable energy the grid couldn't absorb or move, reported as regional volumes by the TSOs (netztransparenz, 1–3 day lag), not per turbine. Negative-price hours are real; curtailment values here are placeholder.
