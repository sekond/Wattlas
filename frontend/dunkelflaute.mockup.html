A Dunkelflaute ("dark doldrums") is a stretch of cold, overcast, windless weather when wind and solar generate almost nothing — often for days. The grid doesn't fail; it leans on firm power (gas, nuclear, hydro) and imports. These hours are why a high-renewable system still needs firm capacity, storage or strong interconnection — not an argument against renewables, but the engineering reality they plan for.
